An Unhealthy Tan

The growing cancer of Earth's decreasing albedo.

Definition of Albedo: According to Earth and Science Research, “Albedo is the fraction of solar energy (shortwave radiation) reflected from the Earth back into space. It is a measure of the reflectivity of the earth's surface.”

To better understand the relationship of albedo and global warming, it is important for us to know that different materials have different albedo, and some of them are quite distinct. Albedo varies between 0 and 1. If the albedo of a specific material is 0, it means that it absorbs all the solar energy and reflects none back, and vice versa.

The composition and physical state of the Earth surface directly affect the Earth’s albedo, therefore it helps explain why there is such a big variance in average temperature in different parts of the world. For example, the surface in Polar Regions is mainly composed by ice, which has an albedo of 70-90%, as a result the surface reflects the majority of solar energy and leads to lower temperature compared to surface composed mainly by seawater, which has an albedo of 10% and absorbs a lot of solar energy.

How does it relate to global warming? It is very clear to see that global warming leads to more ice melting in polar areas. The shrinkage of polar ice cap results in more exposure of seawater; the change of physical state of the surface means a significant decrease of the correspond albedo. When more solar energy is absorbed, the temperature will be increased, thus it intensifies global warming. And then it leads to more ice melting and a vicious cycle is induced.
